# Rottweiler

## Description

The Rottweiler is a large, powerful working dog descended from the drover dogs of ancient Rome. Named after the German town of Rottweil, where they were used to herd livestock and pull butchers' carts, Rottweilers are confident, calm, and courageous dogs with a natural guarding instinct. Despite their imposing appearance, well-socialized Rottweilers are affectionate, playful, and deeply loyal to their families. They are highly intelligent and respond well to firm, consistent training. Rotties excel as police dogs, therapy dogs, and devoted family guardians.

## Diet

Rottweilers are large, muscular dogs that need a protein-rich diet to maintain their strong build. They grow rapidly as puppies, so controlled nutrition is key to preventing joint issues.

- **Puppies (up to 18 months):** 3-4 meals per day of large-breed puppy formula with controlled calcium and phosphorus for steady bone growth
- **Adults (1.5-6 years):** 2 meals per day, approximately 3-5 cups of dry food daily depending on size and activity
- **Seniors (6+ years):** 2 meals per day with joint-support senior formula and moderate calories
- **Key nutrients:** High-quality animal protein (beef, chicken, fish), glucosamine and chondroitin for joints, omega-3 for coat and skin
- **Avoid:** Overfeeding during growth (can cause orthopedic problems), high-fat diets, and free-feeding
