# Bulldog

## Description

The Bulldog (English Bulldog) is a medium-sized, muscular dog with a distinctive wrinkled face and pushed-in nose. Originally bred in England for the cruel sport of bull-baiting in the 13th century, the modern Bulldog has been selectively bred for a calm, courageous, and friendly temperament since the sport was banned in 1835. Today, Bulldogs are gentle, dignified, and affectionate companions known for their loyalty and love of lounging. Their unmistakable appearance and easygoing personality have made them a beloved household pet and a popular mascot.

## Diet

Bulldogs have a tendency toward obesity and flatulence, so a carefully managed diet with easily digestible ingredients is essential. Their brachycephalic (flat-faced) structure means they eat slowly and can struggle with large kibble.

- **Puppies (up to 12 months):** 3-4 meals per day of small-kibble puppy formula with moderate protein and fat
- **Adults (1-6 years):** 2 meals per day, approximately 1.5-2 cups of dry food daily; avoid free-feeding
- **Seniors (6+ years):** 2 smaller meals per day with weight-management formula
- **Key nutrients:** Easily digestible proteins (chicken, fish), probiotics for digestive health, omega fatty acids for skin and wrinkle health
- **Avoid:** Overfeeding, high-fat diets, soy and corn (common allergens), and foods that cause gas
