# Golden Retriever

## Description

The Golden Retriever is a large, sturdy sporting dog originally bred in the Scottish Highlands in the mid-19th century for retrieving waterfowl during hunting. Known for their beautiful golden coat, gentle expression, and eager-to-please personality, Golden Retrievers are among the most beloved family dogs in the world. They excel as therapy dogs, assistance dogs, and in obedience and agility competitions. Their patient, friendly, and trustworthy nature makes them exceptional companions for families with children.

## Diet

Golden Retrievers need a balanced diet with quality protein to support their active lifestyle. They love food and are prone to weight gain, so careful portioning is important.

- **Puppies (up to 12 months):** 3-4 meals per day of large-breed puppy formula with moderate fat and controlled growth nutrients
- **Adults (1-7 years):** 2 meals per day, approximately 2-3 cups of dry food daily based on activity level
- **Seniors (7+ years):** 2 meals per day with reduced-calorie formula and added joint support
- **Key nutrients:** Omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids for their thick coat, DHA for brain development in puppies, taurine for heart health
- **Avoid:** Overfeeding, excessive treats, raw salmon (risk of salmon poisoning disease), and high-calorie table scraps
