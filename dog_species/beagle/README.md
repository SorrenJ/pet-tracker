# Beagle

## Description

The Beagle is a small-to-medium scent hound developed in England primarily for tracking hare, rabbit, and other small game. With a history dating back to Roman times, the modern Beagle was refined in the 1830s and became one of the most popular companion breeds. Beagles are cheerful, curious, and merry dogs with an extraordinary sense of smell — second only to the Bloodhound. Their compact size, low-maintenance coat, and loving disposition make them excellent family pets, though their strong nose and independent streak can lead to adventurous escapades.

## Diet

Beagles are notoriously food-motivated and highly prone to obesity. Strict portion control and limited treats are essential to keep them at a healthy weight.

- **Puppies (up to 12 months):** 3-4 meals per day of high-quality puppy food with balanced protein and fat
- **Adults (1-7 years):** 2 meals per day, approximately 1-1.5 cups of dry food daily; measure portions carefully
- **Seniors (7+ years):** 2 meals per day with reduced-calorie senior formula
- **Key nutrients:** Lean protein sources (chicken, turkey, fish), fiber for satiety, L-carnitine to support fat metabolism
- **Avoid:** Free-feeding, excessive treats during training, table scraps, and leaving food within reach (Beagles are skilled food thieves)
