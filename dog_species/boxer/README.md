# Boxer

## Description

The Boxer is a medium-to-large, muscular working dog developed in Germany in the late 19th century from the now-extinct Bullenbeisser and English Bulldogs. Originally bred for bull-baiting and later as butcher's helpers and guard dogs, Boxers are playful, energetic, and fiercely loyal companions. Known for their distinctive square head, strong jaw, and expressive face, Boxers are often called the "clowns of the dog world" for their playful antics and joyful spirit. They are patient and protective with children, making them outstanding family dogs. Their alertness and courage also make them natural watchdogs.

## Diet

Boxers are high-energy dogs with sensitive stomachs and a higher-than-average risk of food allergies. They are also a brachycephalic breed, so flat-bottom bowls and appropriately sized kibble help with eating.

- **Puppies (up to 12 months):** 3-4 meals per day of large-breed puppy formula with moderate protein and controlled fat for steady growth
- **Adults (1-7 years):** 2 meals per day, approximately 2-3 cups of dry food daily depending on activity level
- **Seniors (7+ years):** 2 meals per day with senior formula, reduced fat, and added antioxidants
- **Key nutrients:** High-quality animal protein (chicken, beef, fish), L-carnitine and taurine for heart health (Boxers are prone to cardiomyopathy), easily digestible carbohydrates
- **Avoid:** Grains and fillers that trigger allergies, soy-based products, large meals (risk of bloat/GDV — use slow-feeder bowls), and vigorous exercise immediately after eating
