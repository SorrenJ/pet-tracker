# German Shepherd

## Description

The German Shepherd is a large, athletic herding dog that originated in Germany in the late 19th century. Bred by Captain Max von Stephanitz for intelligence and versatility, German Shepherds are renowned for their loyalty, courage, and exceptional trainability. They serve worldwide as police and military dogs, search-and-rescue dogs, guide dogs, and devoted family protectors. Their confident, steady temperament and strong work ethic make them one of the most respected working breeds.

## Diet

German Shepherds require a protein-rich diet to support their muscular build and high energy levels. They can have sensitive stomachs, so consistent, high-quality food is important.

- **Puppies (up to 12 months):** 3-4 meals per day of large-breed puppy formula with controlled calcium for proper bone development
- **Adults (1-7 years):** 2 meals per day, approximately 2.5-3.5 cups of dry food daily depending on activity level
- **Seniors (7+ years):** 2 meals per day with joint-support senior formula and reduced calories
- **Key nutrients:** High-quality animal proteins (chicken, fish, lamb), glucosamine and chondroitin for hip/joint health, probiotics for digestion
- **Avoid:** Rapid diet changes, high-fat foods, corn/soy fillers that may trigger allergies
