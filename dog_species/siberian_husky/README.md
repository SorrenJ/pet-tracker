# Siberian Husky

## Description

The Siberian Husky is a medium-sized working dog bred by the Chukchi people of northeastern Siberia for sled pulling, companionship, and endurance in extreme cold. Known for their striking wolf-like appearance, piercing blue or multicolored eyes, and thick double coat, Huskies are energetic, independent, and remarkably friendly dogs. They gained fame in 1925 during the Great Race of Mercy serum run to Nome, Alaska. Huskies are pack-oriented, vocal (known for howling rather than barking), and have an insatiable love for running. They thrive with active owners who can match their high energy.

## Diet

Siberian Huskies are efficient eaters that historically survived on small amounts of food relative to their size and activity level. They are not typically food-driven and may skip meals when not hungry.

- **Puppies (up to 12 months):** 3-4 meals per day of high-quality puppy food with balanced protein (25-30%) and moderate fat
- **Adults (1-8 years):** 2 meals per day, approximately 1.5-2.5 cups of dry food daily; adjust based on activity (working Huskies need significantly more)
- **Seniors (8+ years):** 2 meals per day with senior formula; Huskies tend to stay lean naturally
- **Key nutrients:** High-quality proteins (fish, chicken), omega-3 fatty acids for their thick double coat, zinc for skin health
- **Avoid:** Overfeeding (they self-regulate well), cheap fillers, and excessive carbohydrates
