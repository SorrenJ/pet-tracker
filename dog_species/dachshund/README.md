# Dachshund

## Description

The Dachshund is a small, elongated hound breed originating from Germany, where its name literally means "badger dog." Bred over 300 years ago to hunt badgers, rabbits, and other burrowing animals, the Dachshund's distinctive long body and short legs were purposefully developed to follow prey into underground tunnels. Available in two sizes (standard and miniature) and three coat types (smooth, wirehaired, and longhaired), Dachshunds are bold, curious, and surprisingly brave for their size. Their playful, stubborn personality and comical appearance have made them one of the most recognizable and popular small dog breeds worldwide.

## Life Span

12 - 16 years

## Diet

Dachshunds are prone to obesity, which is particularly dangerous for their long spines and can lead to intervertebral disc disease (IVDD). Maintaining a lean body weight is critical for their health.

- **Puppies (up to 12 months):** 3-4 small meals per day of high-quality puppy food; miniatures may need tiny, frequent meals
- **Adults (1-8 years):** 2 meals per day; Standard: 1-1.5 cups, Miniature: 0.5-0.75 cups of dry food daily
- **Seniors (8+ years):** 2 meals per day with weight-management senior formula
- **Key nutrients:** Lean proteins, glucosamine for spine and joint support, calcium for bone health, fiber for digestive regularity
- **Avoid:** Overfeeding (even a small weight gain stresses their spine), jumping on/off furniture after meals, high-calorie treats, and foods that cause bloating
