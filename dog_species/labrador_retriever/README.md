# Labrador Retriever

## Description

The Labrador Retriever is a medium-to-large, strongly built sporting dog originating from Newfoundland, Canada. Originally bred to assist fishermen in retrieving nets and catching fish, Labs are known for their friendly, outgoing, and high-spirited temperament. They are one of the most popular dog breeds worldwide, prized for their intelligence, gentle nature, and versatility as family companions, service dogs, and working dogs.

## Diet

Labrador Retrievers thrive on high-quality commercial or home-prepared dog food appropriate to their age (puppy, adult, or senior). They are prone to obesity, so portion control is essential.

- **Puppies (up to 12 months):** 3-4 meals per day of puppy-formula food rich in protein (25-30%) and healthy fats
- **Adults (1-7 years):** 2 meals per day, approximately 2-3 cups of dry food daily depending on activity level
- **Seniors (7+ years):** 2 meals per day with reduced-calorie senior formula to maintain healthy weight
- **Key nutrients:** Omega-3 fatty acids for coat health, glucosamine for joint support, lean proteins
- **Avoid:** Overfeeding, table scraps, grapes, chocolate, onions, and foods high in fat
